# Consent to Participate in Research

## Study Title
Investment Decision-Making Under Uncertainty

## Purpose
You are being invited to participate in a research study about how people make financial investment decisions when presented with different types of information. The study examines how chart visualizations and contextual information affect decision-making.

## What You Will Do
- Answer a few background questions about your risk preferences and financial knowledge.
- Complete 2 short practice rounds to become familiar with the task.
- View 32 investment charts, each showing a projected asset performance, and decide whether you would prefer to invest in the charted asset or a fixed-odds alternative.
- Answer a brief demographic survey and provide optional feedback.

The study takes approximately **15–20 minutes** to complete.

## Risks and Benefits
There are no known risks beyond those encountered in everyday life. The study involves hypothetical investment decisions only — **no real money is at stake**. Your participation will contribute to research on how people interpret financial visualizations.

## Compensation
You will be compensated as described in the study posting on the recruitment platform.

## Confidentiality
Your responses will be collected anonymously and stored securely. No personally identifying information will be linked to your responses. Data will be used for academic research purposes only.

## Voluntary Participation
Your participation is entirely voluntary. You may withdraw at any time without penalty by closing your browser window. If you withdraw, your partial data may still be used in aggregate analyses.

## Contact
If you have questions about this study, please contact the research team at **researcher@university.edu**.
